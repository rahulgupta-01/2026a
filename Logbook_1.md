# 2026 PP5-SCH Secondary TCHR5007 Professional Experience Immersion Program Logbook

**TCHR5007 - Professional Experience I - Secondary**

## Aim of the School Immersion Program

The aim of this placement is for you to observe the ways that schools and teachers implement the curriculum to cater for diverse learners. Throughout your course, you have looked at the theory behind teaching for inclusion and ensuring the participation of all students. Whilst visiting your school community for this placement, it is expected that you will reflect on your observations and experiences, linking theory to practice and identifying strategies to implement in your Professional Experience III placement. This experience will provide an opportunity for you to take a good look at the work of teachers, particularly around curriculum and inclusion.

Each day, you will be required to log and reflect on your experiences, using the template provided. You will list the activities, link them to a relevant Australian Professional Standard for Teachers and provide notes about what you have observed. You are then required to reflect on what you have learned about teaching from the experience. These brief reflections should inform your perception of the work of teachers, curriculum, school communities and the profession of teaching in general.

## Immersion Activities

Immersion opportunities will differ between school contexts. However, the checklist below offers a minimum for the expectations of activities for the ten-day placement:

- Become familiar with school policies such as assessment, welfare, health and safety
- Observe assessment design, implementation and moderation
- Assist with marking student work
- Observe the various ways that feedback is provided to students
- Assist with teaching your allocated class
- Observe and discuss approaches to differentiating the curriculum for diverse learners with your mentor teacher and others
- Observe and discuss your mentor teacher’s collection and use of data to inform teaching
- Identify opportunities for incorporating cross-curriculum priorities and general capabilities across lessons/ units of work that you observe
- Discuss with your mentor teacher how they record information about student diversity
- Speak to and/or observe specialist teachers or support staff
- Attend and/or participate in extra-curricular activities (if available)
