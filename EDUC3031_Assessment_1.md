## About this unit

Investigates issues of diversity, equity and inclusion in the Australian education system with a focus on students from backgrounds of cultural diversity, socio-economic disadvantage, rural and remote students, and students from non-English speaking backgrounds. Critically reviews relevant legislation and education policy frameworks for promoting access, participation and success for learners from diverse backgrounds.  Promotes understanding and efficacy in creating culturally safe learning environments, and teaching, learning, and assessment strategies that promote high expectations for all learners.

## Unit learning outcomes

As a graduate of Southern Cross University, you will have developed skills, values and attitudes that are essential for gaining employment and advancing lifelong learning. The University refers to these as graduate attributes.

### Learning outcomes for this unit

- critically examine factors and issues and the relationships between culture, language, identity and history influencing students’ learning experiences in Australian educational settings
- evaluate current national and state legislation and educational policies aimed at improving access, participation and success within the Australian education system students from culturally diverse backgrounds; socio-economic backgrounds; rural and remote students; and non-English speaking backgrounds and refugee circumstances, and assess their implications for the profession
- demonstrate sound knowledge of the attitudes and cultural competencies required of teachers to provide culturally safe and inclusive learning environments for learners from diverse cultural backgrounds, including the EAL/D dimension of their learning
- evaluate curriculum, teaching, learning and assessment strategies that promote success for students from diverse cultural and socio-economic backgrounds; rural and remote students; and students with EAL/D, and the pedagogical skills to successfully support students’ learning

## Rationale

This section describes the purpose of the assessment task (the ‘why’).

Teachers need to be able to identify students’ individual and diverse needs and then plan for how they will meet those needs. This task supports your ability to apply inclusive pedagogical frameworks in real-world planning, ensuring that all students can access and engage with learning.

## Task Description:

This section provides a brief description of the assessment task (the ‘what’).

Select one of the provided Student Scenarios and develop an Individual Education Plan (IEP) for the student described. Your IEP should include teaching and learning adjustments/accommodations tailored to the student's needs, and a detailed justification for each adjustment. Use the provided IEP Template.

## Instructions

### Part A: Use the IEP scaffold provided and fill it in

Identify the student’s specific needs across cognitive, social, emotional and or behavioural.

Clearly identify the teaching and learning adjustments/accommodations you have made for this student.

Ensure your adjustments reflect inclusive pedagogical principles (e.g., Universal Design for Learning, differentiation, trauma-informed practice).

### Part B: Justification 

Explain and justify why you have selected each adjustment.

Demonstrate your understanding of inclusive pedagogical principles and how they inform your planning.

Support your reasoning with relevant academic literature.

## Referencing

You are to use the APA7 style for citations and referencing.

You can use GenAI in all possible ways. There are absolutely no restriction on using GenAI tools to complete this assessment in full.

# Student Scenarios that you need to use for this assignment:

## Secondary Scenario: Rebeka (Year 8, Aboriginal student moved from remote to urban)

## Student Background:

Rebeka recently moved from a remote community in Wilcannia to an urban school in Coffs Harbour. She lives with extended family and maintains a strong connection to her culture and language. Until the move to Coffs Harbour, Rebeka mainly spoke Barkindji dialect at home.

## Narrative:

Rebeka enjoys sports and is confident in oral communication, often sharing stories about her community. In her new school, Rebeka is adjusting to different routines and expectations. She participates in practical activities but hesitates during tasks that involve writing or formal presentations.

Her teacher observes that Rebeka understands concepts well but struggles to express ideas in writing. She sometimes avoids eye contact when asked to read aloud and prefers working with peers she trusts. Outside school, Rebeka attends cultural events and values her identity.

She has said she likes PE and art but finds writing difficult. While she is making friends, she occasionally feels isolated when classroom tasks rely heavily on written English, which makes her anxious.

# Secondary Scenario: Wendy (Year 10, EAL/D, Gifted and Talented Learner from China)

## Student Background:

Wendy moved to Australia from China two years ago and speaks Mandarin and Cantonese at home. She is diligent and highly motivated, with strong skills in mathematics and music. Her family values academic success and encourages her to excel.

## Narrative:

Wendy enjoys learning and consistently performs well in subjects that involve numbers or formulas. She is considered gifted in mathematics and music, often completing complex tasks quickly and accurately.

However, Wendy finds it challenging to understand nuanced English, especially in subjects requiring extended writing or abstract discussion.

She rarely asks questions, worried about making mistakes, and spends extra time checking her work. In group activities, Wendy participates but tends to let others lead conversations.

At home, she revises notes and practices English but still feels nervous about oral presentations and essay writing. Wendy has expressed that she wants to do well but sometimes struggles with instructions due to unfamiliar vocabulary.

---

# Secondary Scenario: Jimmy (Year 9, refugee background from South Sudan)

## Student Background:

Jimmy arrived in Australia after spending several years in a refugee camp in Kenya. He lives with his mother and siblings and is known for his friendly and outgoing nature.

## Narrative:

Jimmy loves sports and practical activities, often volunteering to help with hands-on tasks. In the classroom, Jimmy enjoys group work but can become restless during long lessons.

His spoken English has improved significantly, but reading and writing remain difficult for him.

He sometimes becomes frustrated when he cannot complete tasks quickly and has had a few incidents of challenging behaviour. His teacher suspects these moments may be linked to past trauma and feelings of being overwhelmed.

Jimmy wants to succeed and often says he likes school, especially PE. However, he avoids tasks that involve extended reading and writing, and he struggles to organise his work.

Despite these challenges, Jimmy is eager to learn and responds well to encouragement and practical activities.
