## About this unit
Introduces a holistic approach to classroom management designed to guide teachers in promoting a positive and constructive learning environment in schools and other educational settings.

Teachers bring their understanding of students' development and characteristics of students and how these may affect learning.

Analysis, evaluation and application of classroom management concepts, processes, and skills, required to teach are addressed to promote positive group and individual behaviour in students for learning.

### Modules
1. Student Engagement
2. Connecting with Learners
3. Teaching for Impact
4. Behaviour Management
5. Cultural Competence
6. Planning for Classroom Management

## Unit learning outcomes
As a graduate of Southern Cross University, you will have developed skills, values and attitudes that are essential for gaining employment and advancing lifelong learning. The University refers to these as graduate attributes.

### Learning outcomes for this unit
1. demonstrate a knowledge of existing institutional policies relating to classroom management of individual and group social behaviour, and the effect these policies have on the practice of behaviour management
2. demonstrate knowledge of classroom management regarding whole person approach, and the significance of the learning environment including innovative teaching strategies and routines
3. analyse the roles and responsibilities of teachers in classroom management, in partnership with parents
4. identify and apply proactive processes that lead to student engagement and self-regulation including social skills training, conflict resolution, and cooperation for classroom management

## Task Overview

This task simulates a graduate teacher interview in which you present a lesson designed for a diverse class. Your presentation must demonstrate how your planning, instructional decisions, and behaviour-support strategies are informed by relevant policy, educational theory, and whole-person approaches to classroom management. You should show how these influences shape the learning environment, support student participation, engagement, and positive behaviour, and guide your professional decision-making in partnership with parents and carers.

You will design one lesson that responds to the diverse needs outlined in the supplied classroom scenario, including students with disability, social-emotional needs, behaviour support requirements, low-level disruption, escalation, and cyberbullying. In your presentation, you will explain how and why you have designed specific instructional adjustments, behaviour supports, and modifications, referencing relevant policies and research to justify your decisions.

Your narrated presentation will be uploaded to YouTube as UNLISTED and the link placed on the cover page or at the top of the second page in the Assignment and will:

- present and justify one lesson that responds to the diverse learning and behavioural needs described in the scenario
- explain the adjustments, supports, routines, transitions, and communication strategies used to create a safe, inclusive, and engaging learning environment
- connect your decisions to relevant policy, theory, and evidence throughout

This task requires you to prepare for an interview for a teaching position in a school. You are to design and present a Narrated Presentation that demonstrates your understanding of classroom management in a specific, scenario-based context.

## Your Class Scenario

### School Context

You are a classroom teacher at a government school in a rapidly growing suburban area of western Sydney. The school caters for students from Kindergarten to Year 10 and operates a Positive Behaviour for Learning (PBL) framework with three schoolwide expectations: Be Kind, Be Brave, Be Your Best. The student population has grown significantly in the past five years and reflects the area's cultural diversity, with over 20 language backgrounds represented and approximately half of all students speaking a language other than English at home.

The school draws from a mix of social housing, new estates, and established suburbs. It participates in the Nationally Consistent Collection of Data (NCCD) and has a wellbeing team that includes a school counsellor, learning support coordinator, and community liaison officer.

Your class includes students from a range of cultural and linguistic backgrounds, with approximately one-third speaking a language other than English at home. ## Task Instructions

Your presentation is structured in two parts and should run for approximately 10 minutes in total. The focus of this task is to design one well-planned, well-justified lesson.

### Part A: Context Analysis (approximately 3 to 4 minutes)

In this section, analyse the class scenario and establish the foundation for your lesson design. Demonstrate that you can identify who these students are, what they need, and the level of adjustment required, and explain how relevant policy and professional responsibilities guide your decisions as a classroom teacher. In Part A, you are identifying needs and planning your approach, do not describe specific strategies here, save those for Part B.

Address the following components:

### 1. Learner Needs

Identify the key learning and behavioural needs across the class, with specific attention to each named student: Amara (disability and access), Zara (anxiety and selective mutism), Ethan (cyberbullying and behaviour support), and Ryan (low-level disruption and escalation risk).

For the following Tier 2 students, identify which areas of differentiation may be needed to support their learning and behaviour and briefly justify your thinking:

- Ethan (Tier 2: cyberbullying and behaviour support)
- Ryan (Tier 2: low-level disruption and escalation risk)

For the following Tier 3 students, identify the level of adjustment required using the NCCD framework (QDTP, Supplementary, Substantial, or Extensive) and briefly justify your decision. Note any documented plans such as an IEP that will inform your lesson design in Part B:

- Amara (Tier 3: disability and access)
- Zara (Tier 3: anxiety and selective mutism)

Consider what might need to be differentiated across content (what students learn and how they access it), process (the activities through which students make sense of ideas), product (how students demonstrate their understanding), and environment (the physical or social conditions that support learning). Specific strategies will be demonstrated in Part B.

### 2. Policy Alignment

Identify at least three policy documents and explain how they shape your professional responsibilities for this class. Connect your planning to relevant national, state, and school-level policy. Your selection must include at least one Commonwealth and one NSW document or document from your chosen state or territory context.

### 3. Universal Classroom Foundations

Outline the proactive, universal strategies you will establish to prevent disruption, build a positive climate, and create a predictable, inclusive environment for all 26 students. This section sets up the Tier 1 context for your lesson design in Part B.

## Part B: Lesson Walkthrough (approximately 6 to 7 minutes)

In this section, present your lesson and provide a clear rationale for how it addresses learner needs and supports a safe, inclusive, and engaging learning environment. Walk the panel through your lesson, explaining and justifying your planning decisions rather than simply describing them. Clearly justify how your strategies support positive behaviour, student engagement, and safe learning environments. Your lesson must address the five areas below. Each area maps directly to a criterion and a slide in your presentation:

### Area 1: Lesson Design and Explicit Instruction

Present the structure and flow of your lesson. Show clear routines and smooth transitions that maximise learning time, increase predictability, and actively support student participation and engagement. Explain the explicit instructional practices you have built in to reduce cognitive load and support focus. Describe your learning goals and explain how you will monitor student progress during and after the lesson. Identify the evidence you will use to determine whether students are meeting the learning goals and explain how this evidence would inform your next teaching decisions. Explain how the MTSS framework organises your lesson, showing how universal practices, targeted supports, and individualised adjustments are embedded in the lesson design.

### Area 2: Student Adjustments and Inclusive Practice

Explain the practical adjustments required for students with disability or additional learning needs. Explain how you apply the MTSS framework across all three tiers in your lesson design. Describe the universal practices embedded for all students, the targeted supports for Ethan and Ryan, and the individualised adjustments for Amara and Zara. Explain how you will monitor and review the effectiveness of these supports using evidence of engagement and progress and explain how this evidence would guide adjustments to your teaching or support strategies to inform future planning.

### Area 3: Positive Behaviour, Relationships and Communication

Explain how your lesson promotes positive student behaviour and respectful peer interactions. Describe the verbal and non-verbal communication strategies you will use to support participation. Your lesson sequence must demonstrate effective communication with parents, carers, and the wider community. Explain how and when you will communicate with the parents and carers of each named student, addressing the different communication needs of each family (for example, Amara's parents, Ethan's separated households, Ryan's shift-working mother, Zara's parents' concern about misinterpretation).

### Area 4: Proactive and Inclusive Approaches

Explain your use of early intervention strategies to manage low-level disruption and support student self-regulation (for example, pre-corrections, redirection, de-escalation). Demonstrate engagement with the school's whole-school positive behaviour framework by showing how the shared expectations (Be Kind, Be Brave, Be Your Best), consistent language, and school-wide processes are embedded in your lesson design. Explain how your lesson addresses cyberbullying both preventatively and in response to Ethan's specific situation, in a way that is restorative and inclusive.

### Area 5: Contingency Reflection

Identify one moment in your lesson where a planned strategy might not work as intended (for example, a de-escalation approach that does not reduce Ryan's frustration, or an unexpected transition that triggers Zara's anxiety). Explain what you would do instead, why you would make this professional decision, and how this alternative response would better support student behaviour, engagement, or wellbeing. This section demonstrates your capacity for adaptive professional judgement.
